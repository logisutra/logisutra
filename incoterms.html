Step 3: Understanding Incoterms

What are Incoterms?
Incoterms are international shipping rules that define who pays for transport and who is responsible if goods are damaged.
EXW (Ex Works)

The Rule: The buyer picks up the goods directly from your factory. You have zero shipping responsibility.

FOB (Free On Board)

The Rule: You pay to bring the goods to the ship/airport in your country. The moment it's loaded, responsibility transfers to the buyer.

CIF (Cost, Insurance & Freight)

The Rule: You pay for the entire ocean/air shipping and insurance all the way to the buyer's destination port.
