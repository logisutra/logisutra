Business Setup Checklist

Follow these detailed steps to establish a legally compliant export business:

Choose a legal structure (Sole Proprietorship, Partnership, or LLC). You must register with your corporate registry to obtain an official Business Registration Certificate.

Do not use a personal account. Approach a commercial bank to open a Current Account that supports foreign currency transactions (USD, EUR) and can receive international SWIFT wires.

Register with your national Customs authority. You will need your local Tax Identification Number to apply for your dedicated Import-Export Code (IEC).

Every product traded internationally has an 8-digit Harmonized System (HS) Code. This code determines the exact customs duty, taxes, and rules in your buyer's country.

Incoterms Cheat Sheet

International shipping regulations defining responsibility maps:

EXW (Ex Works)

Buyer picks up goods from your factory floor. Minimal risk to you.

FOB (Free On Board)

You cover costs until delivery onto the shipping vessel. Common benchmark.

CIF (Cost, Insurance & Freight)

You pay freight and insurance to the destination port.
