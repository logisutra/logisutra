GST & Tax Rules for Exporters

Exporting is highly incentivized. Governments treat international sales as "Zero-Rated Supplies", meaning you do not pay domestic tax on goods sent out of the country.

1. Export Under LUT (Letter of Undertaking)

The most common method. You file a free document called an LUT online once a year. This allows you to ship goods across borders completely tax-free without blocking your working capital. Click here to file your LUT on the GST Portal.

2. Export With IGST Payment & Refund

You pay the integrated tax upfront when shipping, and then claim a 100% refund from the government customs portal once the vessel leaves. Great if you want to clear accumulated input tax credits quickly. Click here to track your IGST Customs Refunds.

3. Verify Your Product's GST Rate

Different commodities attract different tax percentages (0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, or 28%). You must align your product's HS code with the correct tax schedule to prevent processing penalties. Click here to check the official CBIC Tax Rate finder.

4. Claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) on Raw Materials

Even if your final export is tax-free, you paid local taxes on your packaging, raw components, or manufacturing. You can legally claim a cash refund for those domestic business expenses. Click here to file an ITC Refund Application.

Incoterms Cheat Sheet

International shipping regulations defining responsibility maps:

EXW (Ex Works)

Buyer picks up goods from your factory floor. Minimal risk to you.

FOB (Free On Board)

You cover costs until delivery onto the shipping vessel. Common benchmark.

CIF (Cost, Insurance & Freight)

You pay freight and insurance to the destination port.

Customs Clearance Workflow

Before your cargo goes onto an international container ship or airplane, it must pass national customs inspection.

The Shipping Bill is Key

Your Customs Broker will submit a document called a Shipping Bill electronically. Customs officers review your Commercial Invoice, Packing List, and HS Code against this bill to clear your goods.

The Icegate / Customs Portal Tracking

Once cleared, you receive a 'Let Export Order' (LEO). This is the green light that allows your container to physically load onto the ship.
