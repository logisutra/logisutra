GST & Tax Rules for Exporters

Exporting is highly incentivized. Governments treat international sales as "Zero-Rated Supplies", meaning you do not pay domestic tax on goods sent out of the country.

1. Export Under LUT (Letter of Undertaking)

The most common method. You file a free document called an LUT online once a year. This allows you to ship goods across borders completely tax-free without blocking your working capital. Click here to file your LUT on the GST Portal.

2. Export With IGST Payment & Refund

You pay the integrated tax upfront when shipping, and then claim a 100% refund from the government customs portal once the vessel leaves. Great if you want to clear accumulated input tax credits quickly. Click here to track your IGST Customs Refunds.

3. Verify Your Product's GST Rate

Different commodities attract different tax percentages (0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, or 28%). You must align your product's HS code with the correct tax schedule to prevent processing penalties. Click here to check the official CBIC Tax Rate finder.

4. Claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) on Raw Materials

Even if your final export is tax-free, you paid local taxes on your packaging, raw components, or manufacturing. You can legally claim a cash refund for those domestic business expenses. Click here to file an ITC Refund Application.

🚢 Incoterms Quick Reference Guide

International Commercial Terms (Incoterms) define the precise legal responsibilities between a buyer and a seller during international shipping.

What are Incoterms?
Incoterms are international shipping rules that define who pays for transport and who is responsible if goods are damaged or lost. They dictate exactly where liability and risk transfer from the exporter to the importer.
EXW (Ex Works)

The Rule: The buyer picks up the goods directly from your factory or warehouse door. You have zero shipping liability or transport costs once they leave your property.

FOB (Free On Board)

The Rule: You (the exporter) are responsible for domestic transport, port handling, and clearing export customs. The risk and cost transfer to the buyer the exact moment the goods cross the ship's rail at the port.

CIF (Cost, Insurance & Freight)

The Rule: You must pay for the ocean freight delivery and marine insurance all the way to the buyer's destination port. However, your risk of damage technically ends once the goods are safely loaded on the vessel at your home port.

🛂 Customs Clearance Hub

Track your clearance workflow, verify mandatory shipping documents, and estimate your customs incentives.

1. Filing the Shipping Bill

Your Customs Broker (CHA) submits a Shipping Bill electronically via ICEGATE. Customs evaluates your Commercial Invoice and HS Codes to determine if your container falls into the automatic green channel or requires verification.

2. Port Assessment & Inspection

Once the cargo arrives at the port's Container Freight Station (CFS), officers review physical parameters. If flagged, they cross-examine physical quantities against your digital invoice declarations.

3. Let Export Order (LEO)

The grand finale! Once satisfied, customs issues the Let Export Order (LEO). This is the legal green light allowing the crane crew to physically load your cargo onto the vessel.
