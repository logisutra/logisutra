International Commercial Terms (Incoterms)

Select your business's current operational perspective to filter financial liabilities and shipping risk boundaries.

FOB Free On Board (Named Port of Shipment)

Risk transfers completely the moment the goods safely clear the ship's rail at the originating shipping port.

Your Responsibility as the Exporter: You pay to bring goods to the port and load them onto the vessel. Once they are on the ship, you are free of liability and can legally claim your export payment.
Your Responsibility as the Importer: You pay for ocean freight transportation, marine cargo transit insurance, destination customs port clearance, and final localized trucking.
CIF Cost, Insurance & Freight (Named Port of Destination)

Seller pays freight and basic marine insurance up to the buyer's destination port, but risk transfers once cargo is loaded at source.

Your Responsibility as the Exporter: You must buy cargo insurance and arrange ocean freight delivery up to the destination harbor. Your cost calculation metrics must factor in international ocean transit fees.
Your Responsibility as the Importer: Your shipping costs start directly at your local arrival port. You are completely liable for domestic customs clearance and unloading logistics.
EXW Ex Works (Named Place of Delivery)

Maximum obligation falls squarely on the buyer. Seller simply makes goods available at their factory door step.

Your Responsibility as the Exporter: Minimum obligation. You simply pack the items safely inside your domestic warehouse. You have no duty to handle local port trucking or export customs clearances.
Your Responsibility as the Importer: You bear 100% of all expenses and risks. You must hire local trucks inside the exporter's country, manage their foreign customs export clearings, and handle the ocean logistics.
